# XTE J1118+480 — MIR origin debate

References behind the competing explanations of the J1118 quiescent MIR excess (extended hot flow vs. compact jet vs. circumbinary dust). The P1 entries are the three load-bearing references in my paper's SSA section — each why-line states the specific claim in the draft that the paper must actually support, so read for that claim.

## P1 — read soon

- [ ] **Yuan & Narayan 2014** — *Hot Accretion Flows Around Black Holes* (ARA&A 52, 529) — backs the ADAF exclusion: verify the ν_peak ∝ M^(−1/2) ṁ^(1/2) scaling and the ~0.8 µm peak for M ~ 7 M⊙ — my single most-exposed quantitative claim — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1401.0586)
- [ ] **Veledina+2013** — *Hot accretion flow in black hole binaries: a link connecting X-rays to the infrared* (MNRAS 430, 3196) — backs "single SSA zone = jet base or extended hot flow": verify a hot-flow self-absorbed synchrotron component can peak in the MIR in quiescence — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1210.0236)
- [ ] **Poutanen & Veledina 2014** — *Modelling Spectral and Timing Properties of Accreting Black Holes: The Hybrid Hot Flow Paradigm* (Space Sci. Rev. 183, 61) — companion review for the same claim; the hybrid hot-flow picture in full — [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2014SSRv..183...61P)
- [ ] **Plotkin+2015** — *Constraints on relativistic jets in quiescent black hole X-ray binaries from broad-band spectral modelling* (MNRAS 446, 4098) — verify what it says **about J1118 specifically** (jet supplies the IR if its break is below the IR band; CBD as viable alternative) — Plotkin is a co-author and will notice — [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2015MNRAS.446.4098P)
